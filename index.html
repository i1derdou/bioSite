Survivor to Thriver

In September 2015, the life of a young boy, just 13 years old, and those around him changed forever. What started as thoughts of a pulled muscle escalated to the possibility of appendicitis.

Through exploratory surgery, a roughly baseball-sized mass was discovered. Alongside this daunting discovery, almost 3 inches of the small intestine, affected by the encroaching cancer, were surgically removed in an attempt to halt its spread. But the challenges did not end there; several blister-like spots were also identified within the stomach cavity, suggesting that the cancer had begun to metastasize. Through a biopsy, what we thought was cancer now had a name: Burkitt's Lymphoma. Burkitts is an aggressive non-Hodgkin B-cell lymphoma and is known as one of the fastest-growing forms of cancer.

After an overnight stay in the hospital, he was flown to Memphis, Tennessee, to St Jude Children's Research Hospital, known for its groundbreaking research and treatment in pediatric oncology, to begin treatment. His first round of chemotherapy was started on September 10, 2015, his 14th birthday. Over the next three and a half months, St. Jude Children's Research Hospital became more than just a place of treatment for him; it became his home.

This young man's story echoes the experiences of many who have faced similar battles. It serves as a powerful reminder of life's unpredictable nature, the importance of compassion and medical innovation, and the unquenchable spirit of those who fight to overcome life's most difficult challenges.
