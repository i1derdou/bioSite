About

Tanner's treatment was over on December 25th, 2015. Almost a decade later, he continues his life as a survivor.

Although the diagnosis of cancer sounds like it should be a person's only life focus, it is only a tiny part of who they are and not the sum of one's life. This is especially true for Tanner.

Cancer was a diagnosis but not a sentence. Tanner is a brother and now a husband. He is a son, an uncle, a college graduate, and a business owner. Tanner loves Jesus and others with a tenacity that is more significant than most because he knows that life is short, which makes it valuable and unique.

He beat cancer, but unfortunately, his mom was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2016, and after a long battle, she passed away in October of 2017, shortly after his 16th birthday. This was a setback after seeing his mom going through the same treatments that he went through a short time before. But Tanner didn't let this stop him. He finished high school at the top of his class, marrying his high-school sweetheart in October 2023. He will graduate college in May 2024 with a degree in Bio-Chemistry and Biology. His next adventure is medical school, where he hopes to become an oncologist and maybe even cure cancer someday.

No matter what life may put in his way, Tanner will meet the challenges head-on and succeed. After all, he has gone from being a survivor to becoming a thriver.
